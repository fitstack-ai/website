# Week 1 Drafts — @mattmasteller

## Day 1: The Thesis (Pin this one)

**Post:**
Interfaces come and go. Data remains.

I've spent 20+ years building platforms across fitness, fintech, and enterprise. The one constant: companies that own their data win. Companies that rent it through someone else's UI eventually lose.

AI agents are about to prove this at scale. When there's no human clicking buttons, the only thing that matters is your data layer.

Build accordingly.

## Day 2: The SaaS Reckoning

**Post:**
$1 trillion in enterprise software value vanished in a single week.

Not because AI is killing software. Because AI is exposing which software actually owns the value chain — and which is just a pretty interface sitting on top of someone else's data.

The market is finally pricing in what builders have known for years.

## Day 3: Builder POV

**Post:**

Fix your data layer first. Then the AI stuff gets easy.

**Post:**

Fitness → fragmented vendor stacks, nobody owns the member data
Fintech → embedded finance eating traditional banking UIs
Crypto → protocols don't care what frontend you use

The consistent winner? Whoever controls the data and context layer.

Interfaces are a commodity. Context is a moat.

## Day 7: Weekend Thread (longer form)

**Thread:**
🧵 Why I think 2026 is the year "platform" actually means something again:

1/ For a decade, "platform" became a meaningless buzzword. Every SaaS with an API called itself a platform. Most were just apps with webhooks.

2/ AI agents are changing what "platform" actually requires. An agent doesn't care about your UI. It needs structured data, clear APIs, and contextual intelligence.

3/ This means real platforms — the ones with genuine data layers and domain-specific context — suddenly have a massive advantage over UI-first competitors.

4/ The companies that invested in boring stuff (data models, integrations, domain expertise) are about to look like geniuses.

5/ The ones that invested in pretty dashboards and "delightful UX"? They're the $1T that vanished from public markets last month.

6/ I'm building in this space across fitness and fintech. The pattern is identical in both. Own the data layer, own the future.

Interfaces come and go. Data remains.
