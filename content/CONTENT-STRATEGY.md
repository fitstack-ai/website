# Twitter Content Strategy

## Two Voices

### @mattmasteller — The Builder
**Position:** Tech executive, AI-native platform thinker, multi-industry builder
**Tone:** Direct, confident, opinionated. Speaks from experience, not theory.
**Content mix:**
- 40% AI + business transformation
- 25% Fitness/wellness industry insights
- 20% Fintech / crypto / investing
- 15% Builder POV / CTO lessons

**Voice rules:**
- First person, conversational
- Reference real experience ("After 20 years building platforms...")
- Contrarian takes welcome — challenge conventional wisdom
- Short and punchy > long and rambling
- No corporate speak, no buzzword salads
- Okay to be provocative

### @fitstack_ai — The Company
**Position:** AI-native operating infrastructure for the fitness industry
**Tone:** Authoritative, forward-looking, industry-specific. Less personal, more institutional.
**Content mix:**
- 50% Fitness industry + technology insights
- 25% AI/data infrastructure (applied to fitness)
- 15% Industry news commentary
- 10% Company updates / milestones (when ready)

**Voice rules:**
- Third person or "we"
- More polished than Matt's personal account
- Focus on the industry problem, not self-promotion
- Repost Matt's relevant personal posts with company context
- Educational tone — teach operators something

## Cadence

### Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2): Foundation
- **@mattmasteller:** 1 original post/day + 3-5 strategic replies
- **@fitstack_ai:** 3 posts/week + repost Matt's relevant content

### Phase 2 (Weeks 3-4): Momentum  
- **@mattmasteller:** 1-2 posts/day + threads + replies
- **@fitstack_ai:** 5 posts/week

### Phase 3 (Month 2+): Full presence
- Both accounts active daily
- Cross-pollination between accounts
- Engagement with industry accounts
